The Life Cycle of a Star

Elements of the stars

Many of the different types of stars in our solar system are made of elements.

Many of the different types of elements are Hydrogen, Helium, Oxygen, Carbon, Nitrogen, Neon, Magnesium, Silicon, Sulfur, Iron, Silver, Gold, Oxygen, and much more. These different types of elements vary on each stage of a star. But it's amazing to see the different types of elements that are formed. Over the course of this website, you'll see the different types of elements formed and what each element can be used for in Earth.


As you scroll thru the pages, you will see the different types of star formation elements that occurred and what can be made from these elements.

Protostar

A protostar is the first stage of the life cycle of stars. In it contains elements of Hydrogen, Helium, and some other trace elements.


Hydrogen is commonly used in fuel cells to generate electricity, power, or heat. It's also used in petroleum refining and fertilizer production. (energy.gov)

Helium is used in compressed air tanks, lasers (sometimes), coolants, rocket fuel, blimps, and of course... Balloons. (chem4kids.com)

Main Sequence

Main Sequence is the second stage of the life cycle of stars. It contains elements of hydrogen and helium in their cores. Similar to how the Protostar stage contains.


Hydrogen is commonly used in fuel cells to generate electricity, power, or heat. It's also used in petroleum refining and fertilizer production. (energy.gov)

Helium is used in compressed air tanks, lasers (sometimes), coolants, rocket fuel, blimps, and of course... Balloons. (chem4kids.com)

Now let's get into the High Mass Stars...

High Mass Stars have enough mass that it contracts its core and rises its temperature. The core then collapses and releases a large amount of energy in an explosion. (LCO)

There are 3 stages that High Mass Stars can go through. Giant/Supergiant, Supernova, and either the Black Hole or Neutron Star.

Supergiant / Giant

Supergiant star is the third stage in the High Mass Star spectrum. Elements that are formed are iron, hydrogen, helium, oxygen, carbon, nitrogen, neon, magnesium, silicon, and sulfur.


Iron is used to create steel, reinforced concrete, or alloy steel. They can even create bridges, electricity pylons, bicycle chains, and cutting tools. (byjus.com)

Hydrogen is commonly used in fuel cells to generate electricity, power, or heat. It's also used in petroleum refining and fertilizer production. (energy.gov)

Helium is used in compressed air tanks, lasers (sometimes), coolants, rocket fuel, blimps, and of course... Balloons. (chem4kids.com)

Oxygen can be used for compressed oxygen tanks, welding torches, generators, and vehicles. Also, it can be used for breathing. (Sciencing)

Carbon can be used for fuel, plastics, and sometimes black ink for printers and painting. (Ducksters)

Nitrogen is used in ammonia, coolant, oil and petroleum refineries, atmosphere, and soil nutrients (chem4kids.com)

Neon can be found in TV tubes (old television sets only), lasers, and even neon signs. (chem4kids.com)

Magnesium is used in metal alloys and often mixed with aluminum, zinc, silicon, and copper. (Ducksters)

Silicon is used in manufacturing semiconductor chips for electronics and lubricants, greases, and caulks. (Ducksters)

Sulfur is used to make car batteries, fertilizer, and extracting material. It's also used in making products such as cement, detergents, and pesticides. (Ducksters)

Supernova

Supernova is the fourth stage in the High Mass Star spectrum. Elements that are formed are hydrogen and helium.


Hydrogen is commonly used in fuel cells to generate electricity, power, or heat. It's also used in petroleum refining and fertilizer production. (energy.gov)

Helium is used in compressed air tanks, lasers (sometimes), coolants, rocket fuel, blimps, and of course... Balloons. (chem4kids.com)

Neutron Star

Neutron Star is the fifth stage in the High Mass Star spectrum. This is an outcome from the supernova but can either be a Neutron Star or Black Hole. Elements that are formed are strontium, europium, silver, and gold.


Strontium is used in glass for color television cathode ray tubes (for older televisions) and is used to generate electricity for space vehicles. (LiveScience)

Europium is used in scintillators for X-Ray tomography and a source of blue color in light-emitting diodes (LED's). (Britannica)

Silver is commonly used in the manufacture of coins, ornaments, and jewelry. It is also used in fabricating printed electrical circuits. (Britannica)

Gold is used as a conductor of heat and electricity and can be used to make electrical connectors and printed circuit boards. (JLab)

Black Hole

Black Hole is the fifth stage in the High Mass Star spectrum. This is an outcome from the supernova but can be a Neutron Star or Black Hole. Elements that are formed are carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen.


Carbon can be used for fuel, plastics, and sometimes black ink for printers and painting. (Ducksters)

Nitrogen is used in ammonia, coolant, oil and petroleum refineries, atmosphere, and soil nutrients (chem4kids.com)

Oxygen can be used for compressed oxygen tanks, welding torches, generators, and vehicles. Also, it can be used for breathing. (Sciencing)

Now let's get into the Low Mass Stars...

Low Mass Stars consumes its core hydrogen and converts it into helium. It shrinks and heats up gradually and the star usually becomes more luminous. (LCO)

There are 4 stages that Low Mass Stars can go through. Red Giant, Planetary Nebula, White Dwarf, and Black Dwarf.

Red Giant

Red Giant is the third stage that low mass stars go through. Hydrogen, helium, and carbon are formed in the Red Giant.


Hydrogen is commonly used in fuel cells to generate electricity, power, or heat. It's also used in petroleum refining and fertilizer production. (energy.gov)

Helium is used in compressed air tanks, lasers (sometimes), coolants, rocket fuel, blimps, and of course... Balloons. (chem4kids.com)

Carbon can be used for fuel, plastics, and sometimes black ink for printers and painting. (Ducksters)

Planetary Nebula

Planetary Nebula is the fourth stage that low mass stars go through. Carbon, nitrogen, helium, and hydrogen are formed in this stage.


Carbon can be used for fuel, plastics, and sometimes black ink for printers and painting. (Ducksters)

Nitrogen is used in ammonia, coolant, oil and petroleum refineries, atmosphere, and soil nutrients (chem4kids.com)

Helium is used in compressed air tanks, lasers (sometimes), coolants, rocket fuel, blimps, and of course... Balloons. (chem4kids.com)

Hydrogen is commonly used in fuel cells to generate electricity, power, or heat. It's also used in petroleum refining and fertilizer production. (energy.gov)

White Dwarf

White Dwarf is the fifth stage that low mass stars go through. Carbon and oxygen are usually formed in this stage.


Carbon can be used for fuel, plastics, and sometimes black ink for printers and painting. (Ducksters)

Oxygen can be used for compressed oxygen tanks, welding torches, generators, and vehicles. Also, it can be used for breathing. (Sciencing)

Black Dwarf

Black Dwarf is the sixth stage that low mass stars go through. Carbon and oxygen are formed in this stage.


Carbon can be used for fuel, plastics, and sometimes black ink for printers and painting. (Ducksters)

Oxygen can be used for compressed oxygen tanks, welding torches, generators, and vehicles. Also, it can be used for breathing. (Sciencing)
