"What, Exactly, is a geek?"

Geeks are essential to the everyday job that everyone is in. Even though geeks are looked below on and insulted in many ways, they still happen to be successful as everyone uses technology and needs that tech to ensure stability in the workplace.


Geeks by Jon Katz is a story about two boys, Jesse Dailey & Eric Twilegar. The two guys, just getting out of high school, were working at small jobs. Jesse, working at Emco Computers and Eric, working at OfficeMax. Both of them were unsure about their lives. Both of them trying to figure out life, they wanted something interesting. That's when they met Jon Katz. Jon met Jesse from an online messaging service. Jesse was the first member of Geek Club back when he was in high school. He communicated with Jon Katz on anything technology-related including about Geek Club and also talked about his life in the online community and about Eric Twilegar, the second member of Geek Club and roommate of Jesse. Jon Katz found himself, after exchanging phone calls and messages to Jesse, traveling to Idaho to meet Jesse himself.


Upon meeting Jesse Dailey, Jon wanted to know more about him. Jesse showed him to places in Caldwell, Idaho. He saw and explored the life of Jesse like at Emco Computers or that one Chinese Place run by white Mormons. When he got together with Eric Twilegar, he saw that both of them were equally smart in technology world. Jon Katz asked, why he didn't move. Both being suspicious of the idea, Jesse quickly started researching on his computer. He found many jobs where geeks would fit in. Eric, wondering to himself and gaining the courage says, "Jeez, he told Eric. Katz is right. We can go anywhere we want. Let's get the hell out of here." Even though Jon Katz didn't mean that they should leave right now, the situational irony that occurred started an adventure for Jesse and Eric.


So why is the message "Geeks are essential to the everyday job that everyone is in. Even though geeks are looked below on and insulted in many ways, they still happen to be successful as everyone uses technology and needs that tech to ensure stability in the workplace."? The tone is positive in the sentence. It's to exemplify a point where Geeks are successful in the workplace and can succeed in their future. Geeks are smart because they focus on that hobby and have more past experience with it. But the tone becomes negative where you can get insulted. Geeks are insulted and looked down on because we work behind the scenes. No one knows who we are and people can bully us since they've never seen us before. The classification of geeks are people that know their hobby. People that like something, like technology, are the people that succeed more in the future since their career is set to the hobby that is used in the workforce and this makes people knowledgeable since they have been learning this on the site with school and education.
